About Us

Ivanov Transport is among the largest companies in the Bulgarian market of courier services with а market share of over 33%. The company was founded in 1998 and has grown since to a corporate structure with more than 3000 employees and partners and more than 1700 delivery vehicles.

  • Fair remuneration for overtime worked from the first minute!
  • Tax-free bonus payments!
  • A future-proof job with strong opportunities for advancement
  • Work clothing and all technical equipment
  • Full-time, part-time, mini-job, temporary job – depending on your life situation
Job Overview

What we expect from you:

  • Driving a van safely
  • German language skills in speaking and writing
  • Police clearance certificate without entries
  • Friendly dealing with customers
  • Reliability and care when delivering packages

Some of your duties may include:

  • Depending on the site, you may operate powered industrial trucks (PIT) that reaches heights up to 35 feet. Equipment training and certification are provided free of cost.
  • Receive, sort, and put away heavy and bulky inventory
  • Load boxes into trucks for shipment
  • Use technology such as computers and smart phones
  • View prompts on screens and follow direction for some tasks
Job Overview

You'll also need to be able to:

  • Lift up to 49 pounds
  • Use safe practices and methods to handle products heavier than 50 pounds
  • Stand and walk, as well as push, pull, squat, bend, and reach frequently during shifts
  • Use carts, dollies, hand trucks, and other gear to move items around
  • Go up and down stairs (where applicable)

Job Overview

Delivery is our core business. The bags and packages are pre-made by our colleagues in the depot, so that all you have to do is load them in and you can concentrate completely on the delivery. You drive the delivery truck through motorways, country roads and city traffic and deliver the packages to private and business addresses using an automated navigation system.

Job Overview

You’ll be part of the Amazon warehouse team that handles the big stuff – those unique customer orders that weigh over 50 pounds: big screen TVs, furniture, appliances, and more. You’ll be trained on how to use technology to handle these heavy bulk items. Even if you haven’t been a “power” player before, that won’t limit you from gaining valuable job experience because – depending on the site – you may be trained to be a heavy equipment operator, using powered industrial trucks (PIT) such as a forklift or pallet driver. Many shifts will have you at the helm of our power equipment getting orders ready for customers, but other times you’ll be on the warehouse floor of our fulfillment centers, sortation centers, or delivery stations working with your teammates, tackling those hands-on items that need expert handling before they are sent along their delivery route.
